Bulbasaur

Bulbasaur is the first Pokemon in the first Pokedex ever recordered, being given the 001 number. It is a plant based Pokemon and typically was recommended to rookie trainers since it could level up quicker than the rest and was best for new Pokemon trainers. In the games, it evolved into Ivysaur at level 16 and into its third-stage Venusaur at level 32.

Pokedex entries from the original games:

  • (RB)A strange seed was planted on its back at birth. The plant sprouts and grows with this Pokémon.
  • (Y)It can go for days without eating a single morsel. In the bulb on its back, it stores energy.

Charmander

Charmander is the fourth Pokemon in the original Pokedex, being given the number 004. Charmander is a fire-type Pokemon known for being the favorite of many trainers due to it's evolution forms. At level 16, Charmander evolves into Charmelon, and it evolves into its third-stage Charizard at level 36. Charizard learns powerful fire attacks and can learn the 'fly' ability in Pokemon Yellow, making it a valueable addition to the party. Additionally, the Pokemon Champion Lance is known to use Charizard from time to time in his dragon-Pokemon lineup.

Pokedex entries from the original games:

  • (R,B)Obviously prefers hot places. When it rains, steam is said to spout from the tip of its tail.
  • (Y)The flame at the tip of its tail makes a sound as it burns. You can only hear it in quiet places.

Squirtle

Squirtle was the third original starter of the Pokemon series and is listed as 007 on the original Pokedex. Squirtle is a water-type turtle pokemon who was also popular with players, however due to his element type and the early Gym Leader types, he was considered one of the harder Pokemon to start out with. Squirtle evolves into Wartortle at level 16 and evolves into the third-stage Blastoise at level 36 in the original games.

Pokedex entries from the original games:

  • (R,B)After birth, its back swells and hardens into a shell. Powerfully sprays foam from its mouth.
  • (Y)Shoots water at prey while in the water. Withdraws into its shell when in danger.

Pikachu

Pikachu is one of the most popular Pokemon and is popularly known as the mascot of the Pokemon franchise as well as being one of the major Nintendo major mascots. In the games, Pikachu is the starter pokemon in Pokemon Yellow, which closely followed the anime show at the time, and is given the number 025 in the Pokedex. Unlike the other games, you did not get a choice as to who your starter pokemon would be. Pikachu in the original game could not evolve, also following the anime, however in the wild Pikachu evolves to Raichu at any level once being exposed to the Thunder Stone.

Pokex entries from teh original games:

  • (R,B)When several of these Pokémon gather, their electricity could build and cause lightning storms.
  • (Y)It keeps its tail raised to monitor its surroundings. If you yank its tail, it will try to bite you.

Eevee

Eevee is one of the original Pokemon, however until 2018 was not an official starting pokemon for any games that were either first-generation or based off the first-generation. In the original Pokedex, it had the number 133. Upon Let's Go, Eevee! released in 2018, Eevee became a starter Pokemon which was based off Pokemon Yellow, when your rival trainer chooses Eevee. Eevee was originally intended to be your Pokemon in Yellow before being swiped by your rival (hince you getting Pikachu instead). Eevee is a unique Pokemon, in that it can evolve into many different Pokemon based off the stone it is exposed to. In the original games, it evolved either into Vaporeon (Water), Jolteon (Thunder) or Flareon (Fire).

Pokedex entries from the original games:

  • (R,B)Its genetic code is irregular. It may mutate if it is exposed to radiation from Evolution stones.
  • (Y)Its genetic code is unstable, so it could evolve in a variety of ways. There are only a few alive.
